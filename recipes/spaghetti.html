Slightly Fancier Spaghetti and Meatsauce

Putting a little more effort in your spaghetti can go a long way!

Use this recipe to step up from just heating up jar sauce

Jacob taught me this and I think I'm writing it down correctly

Ingredients

  • A jar of your favorite spaghetti sauce
  • A package of noodles
  • 1 lb ground beef (not rolled)
  • 2 cloves of garlic (minced)
  • 1 cup Cooking wine
  • 1 cup Beef broth
  • Seasonings
    • Red Pepper Flakes
    • Salt
    • Italian Seasoning/Oregano
    • Fennel Seads (be careful!)

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a large sauce pan at medium high heat
  2. Add red pepper flakes and garlic cloves and stir for 1 minutes
  3. Add ground beef to skillet as a block
    • Add salt to top and let it sit for a minute
    • Chop up and brown
  4. Once the beef is browned, add the jar of sauce
  5. Add wine and broth
  6. Reduce heat until sauce is consistently simmering
  7. At this point, fill a large pot with water and start to boil it
  8. Add italian seasonings and stir
  9. Mostly cover with lid, leaving a crack to simmer and reduce liquid until sauce is back to its thick texture
  10. While the sauce is simmering and the water in the pot is boiling, add noodles and follow instructions on package
  11. When noodles are done, add a cup of noodle water to the sauce and reduce that, too
  12. Drain noodles in a colander while running cold water over them
  13. When the sauce is reduced sufficiently, add noodles to sauce and stir
  14. The sauce should stick to the noodles
  15. Serve and enjoy!
